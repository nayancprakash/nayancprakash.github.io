Being a member of SPEAR may involve the design, use and/or implementation of various hardware, software, mechanical, and electrical systems. Competing each year against other schools' Mars rovers, this club must also maintain its own finances as well as market itself to additional sponsors.

This year, as a member of the AI team, I will be assisting in developing a Computer Vision system that will allow the Mars rover, TARS, to autonomously navigate its way through unwieldy terrain.

About Me

I first fell in love with computers when I began making electronic music. The fact that I could make something out of thin air using just my own creativity, a keyboard, a mouse and my computer was captivating. The technicality of sound design and automation paired with the expressiveness of chords and melodies drew me in. From here, I ended up custom building my own desktop computer. This meant hours researching what components are required in a computer, figuring out how they all interact and slowly learning the inner workings of computers.

Throughout all of this, my love for math and sciences grew. During high school, physics, chemistry and computer sciences courses were my favourite parts of the day. When it finally came time to apply for University it was my love for math, sciences and computers that drew me into applying to Engineering.

My fascination with the balance between technicality and creativity that initially drew my into music production is the same fascination that drives me through Software Engineering. The ability to create elegant and useful software through technical knowledge and expertise.
